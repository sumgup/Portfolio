MyFitnessStory

Problem

Why is it that 95% of world population have health problems and are unable to give time to there personal Fitness? Whats holding them ?

Approach

This case study is an attempt to look into a problem in completety a new way, took a human centred design approach i.e. Design thinking for understanding and attempting to solve it.

My Role

My role was to drive the case study by recruiting participants, scheduling interviews, defining problem statement, ideating , presenting prototyped solution to users and collecting feedback.

UX Projects or Design thinking do not follow a linear methodology, but for purpose of this case study, I am describing the work done during different phases in linear structure below.

Empathy

To gain empathy about how users think I took the approach of structured and unstructured interview. For gaining deeper understanding I used generative technique i.e. context mapping it helped me understanding day to day life of users.

Recruiting Users

Up for the challenge, I recruited four users who I knew were busy and finding hard to exercise

  • Why do you like to exercise?
  • What are your motivations behind exercise?
  • What are perfect conditions to exercise?
  • Situations and circumstances which prevent exercise
  • Are you happy with current exercise routine?
  • Do you follow any Diet?
  • Are you using any device/app to track fitness?
  • Do you prefer to exercise alone or in a group?

Creating Interview Guide

Interviews should be a mix of planned and unplanned questions, prepared this guide so that I don't forget the important ones.

  • Click photos of what reminds you of exercising
  • Create a timeline of your day
  • Create a Stop Doing list

Probing Kit

I handed this kit to my users so that they can pause and reflect on there lives.

Deliverables

Making point of view actionable by constructing how might we questions.

  • How might we help a busy person outsource there task to create more time for exercise.

  • How might we help a mother to swap task within there family.

  • How might we help a working mother to find a partner to exercise with.

  • How might we help a person who is exercising to keep him/her motivated and give them a affirmative feedback.

  • How might we help people exercise during office hours?

  • How might we help find busy person exercises which can give them quick results in shortest amount of time spent.
